About Blood Donation

Blood donation is a voluntary procedure where a person donates blood to help save lives. Donated blood is used for surgeries, accident victims, cancer patients, and those with blood disorders. A single blood donation can save up to three lives.

How to Protect Yourself

Donate at certified centers, ensure sterile equipment, stay hydrated, eat well, and follow post-donation care.

Before Donation

  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water 24 hours before donating. Avoid caffeinated drinks.
  • Eat a Healthy Meal: Eat iron-rich foods (spinach, eggs, red meat) and avoid fatty foods before donation.
  • Get Enough Rest: Sleep well the night before to keep your body energized.
Read More

During Donation

  • Wear Comfortable Clothing: Prefer sleeves that can be easily rolled up.
  • Relax and Stay Calm: Breathe normally to prevent feeling lightheaded.
  • Inform the Staff: Let the staff know if you feel dizzy or uncomfortable.
  • Follow Hygiene Protocols: Ensure sterile needles and equipment are used.
Read More

After Donation

  • Rest for a Few Minutes: Sit or lie down for 10–15 minutes.
  • Drink Fluids: Continue drinking water and juices to replenish fluids.
  • Eat a Light Snack: Helps restore your energy.
  • Avoid Heavy Lifting: Refrain from strenuous activities for the rest of the day.
